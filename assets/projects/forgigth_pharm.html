üìÅ Dataset Overview

The dataset used for this project is stored in two different Excel workbooks: the Actual Dataset and the Target Dataset.

The Target Dataset contains the following columns: product_id, sales_rep_id, month, and the target years 2022, 2023, and 2025. The values under these year columns represent the expected sales amounts for each sales representative.

The Actual Dataset workbook comprises seven sheets, which include information on supporting dimensions such as products, employees, locations, and channels, as well as the actual sales records for the years 2022 through 2025.

üßπ Data Cleaning and Transformation:

In preparing the dataset for analysis, I first noted that the Actual Sales and Target Sales tables were maintained as two separate fact tables. This was necessary because they exist at different levels of granularity, and therefore establishing a direct relationship between them would not be appropriate.

To streamline the Actual Sales data, I appended the 2023‚Äì2025 sales sheets to the 2022 sales sheet, consolidating them into a single unified table. I then pivoted the year columns (2022, 2023, 2024) into a single column to create a proper structure where each row represents a year with its corresponding values.

after pivot

For the data model, I implemented a star schema, establishing relationships between the fact tables and their corresponding dimension tables. One key challenge arose with the Channel and Subchannel dimensions. The Channel dimension could not be directly related to the fact table, and linking Channel to Subchannel would have violated the star schema principle of dimension-to-fact relationships. To resolve this, I merged Channel and Subchannel into a single dimension table, ensuring a clean and compliant model design.
