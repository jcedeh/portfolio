üìà Key Insights

This analysis explores how student behavior, academic environment, and socio-demographic factors influence JAMB performance. By examining over 5,000 students, we aimed to identify what truly impacts success, and what doesn‚Äôt.

Student Behavior and Academic Habits

The average JAMB score across all students is 174, with an average of 19 study hours per week. The highest score is 367, and the lowest is 100.

Interestingly, while 54.42% of students attend extra tutorials, performance varies significantly based on deeper behavioral and background factors.

Students who study more hours per week tend to score significantly higher, assignment completion and exposure to qualified teachers also correlate strongly with higher scores, students who demonstrate knowledge in IT tend to outperform their peers; signaling that digital literacy may be an academic enabler.

Effort and engagement: measured by study time, IT awareness, and assignment completion, consistently align with academic success.

The Role of the Academic Environment

Private school students have higher average JAMB scores compared to public school students.

Students with shorter distances to school tend to perform better, especially those with very high scores.

Access matters: proximity to school and the quality of the learning environment appear to influence outcomes.

Socio-Demographics and Family Support

Students whose parents have higher education levels tend to achieve better scores.

Parent involvement is another key factor: students with engaged parents perform noticeably better.

What Didn‚Äôt Matter (and Why That‚Äôs Important)

Not all factors we explored were influential. For instance, we found no significant correlation between some commonly assumed drivers like school distance in general (except for very high scorers) or tutorial attendance, access to learning materials, age and gender. This highlights the importance of data validation over assumptions.

üí°Recommendation

To boost JAMB performance at scale, focus should be placed on:

  • Encouraging consistent study habits
  • Improving teacher quality
  • Enhancing parental involvement
  • Promoting IT literacy
  • Addressing socio-economic gaps

These insights provide a foundation for targeted intervention and educational policy reform.
